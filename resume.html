ABOUT ME

I am currently pursuing an undergraduate degree in Architecture at Cornell University. I come from a background of graphic design, traditional art, technology, and social change. I carry forward all of these aspects with me in my design practice, as I continually create bridges between the different branches of knowledge that I engage with in the university.


I am ultimately pursuing a career in Computational Design. My experiences in architecture involved several tools for digital fabrication, parametric design, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Likewise, the classes I have taken outside of my department, including in computer science, game design, and cultural studies, have helped me to see the world through different, yet fundamentally related, ontological lenses. This accumulation of skills and experiences make me excited to work in a field dedicated to exploring design, computers, interactions, and the intersections between fields of knowledge.
