Graphic Design

Here's some artwork I've done from 2019-2022 that I've curated. I've explored a variety of mediums from traditional to digital.

"Salvation" (2019)

I guess I was really panicking during my senior year. I wasn't sure what I'd do with my life after high school—I certainly had my aspirations, but nothing was guaranteed until I knew where I'd go to college. While I waited, I realized everything would be alright, and to express visually this I really dove into this theme of nature taking over human-made objects.

"Abstraction" (2019)

Explores the idea that we don't usually know the in's-and-out's and the inner workings of technology, but that doesn't stop us from using it, and getting addicted to it.

"Daredevil" (2022)

I've been watching Daredevil on Netflix lately. I have a tradition of drawing characters from TV shows and other media that I resonate with or find exceptional.

"Fences" (2020)

Another piece exploring the theme of nature overtaking.

"Gris" (2020)

I fell hopelessly in love with Gris—a platformer so simply presented and yet so emotionally and stylistically impactful. This is the titular character.

"Kaleidoscope" (2019)

I tried to do something in the style of James Jean—an artist, and major inspiration to my own work.

"Life in Ruins" (2020)

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild has to be one of, if not my favorite game of all time. Its atmosphere of overgrowth and nature just came at the right time for me, so here I depicted Link standing right next to a decayed Guardian.

"Root" (2020)

An experiment with gradients in Adobe Illustrator.

"Memento" (2019)

An expressive drawing recounting my visits to 14th Street Union Square in New York City.
